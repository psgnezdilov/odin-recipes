Pulpo a la gallega recipe

Description

Pulpo gallego, or Galician-style octopus, is a popular tapa (appetizer) served all over Spain today. It originated in the northwestern Spanish region of Galicia where octopus (pulpo in Spanish) is a specialty and a common catch for local fishermen.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 pounds octopus, frozen
  • 4 large potatoes
  • 1/4 cup Spanish extra-virgin olive oil, for drizzling
  • Salt, to taste
  • 1 dash sweet Spanish paprika

Steps

  1. Gather the ingredients.
  2. Boil a large pot of water over high heat. Remove 1 1/2 pounds octopus from the freezer and place it directly in the boiling water. Cook until tenderâ€”this usually takes about 1 hour for a 1- to 2-pound octopus. To test its tenderness, insert a knife where the legs and head meet. If it goes in easily, it's ready to eat.
  3. Remove the octopus from the water and allow it to cool. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour.
  4. Cut the chilled meat into bite-sized chunks, slicing the legs in 1/2-inch rounds and the head into thin strips.
  5. Rinse 4 large potatoes and clean with a vegetable brush. Fill a medium-sized pot halfway with water. Bring to a boil over high heat. Boil the potatoes until they can easily be pierced with a fork, about 25 to 25 minutes.
  6. Drain the potatoes and rinse under cold running water. Allow the potatoes to cool, then peel. Slice into rounds approximately 1/3-inch thick.
  7. Arrange the potato slices on a serving platter. Place the octopus on top. Drizzle with 1/2 cup Spanish extra-virgin olive oil. Season with salt and sweet paprika over top and serve.
